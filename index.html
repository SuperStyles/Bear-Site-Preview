How To Make Chicken Stir Fry


Hello there! Today we are going to teach you how to make a dish called Stir Fry Chicken!

When it comes to making food, you always want to be prepared. Don't make any dish you're not prepared to eat, or clean up! This will be a fun activity that engages and makes you feel proud.

Step 1: Gather all your ingredients

First and foremost before you start cooking

Make sure you have all your ingredients together. For this you will need:

  • Chicken Breast
  • Broccoli
  • Bell Peppers
  • Onions
  • Tamari Soy Sauce (or any sauces)
  • Carrots

Note:

These are just the basic ingredients, if you want to you can add any other sauces or ingredients to your dish to make it spazzy. Don't let a set of preset rules make you lose creativity, dive in!

Step 2: Cook & Cut Chicken Breast

To start, first you should put the chicken on an oven-safe pan and set the temperature to about 400 degrees for 22 to 26 minutes. When they reach an internal temperature close to 165 degrees, take them out and rest a minute. Afterwards, cut the chicken into small square pieces (or any shape if you want). Now you have your chicken ready to go.

Now that we got past the more intensive stuff, we can cut to the chase with the choice cuts!

Next you want to boil the broccoli. For this you will likely just need to thaw and let it boil for 3 minutes. If you wish to go further with season and roasting, go right ahead.

Step 3: Boil Broccoli

Getting more simpler! Now you need to cut some bellpeppers to the desired sizes and add them to your dish.

Step 4: Cut The Bellpeppers

Don't cry, this step is just cutting up another side. You will then take the onions and slice them to your desire. You can then season and alter whatever you want.

Step 5: Chop Onions

Keep your eyes out for bugs, because you will now grab some carrots, cut them into smaller sizes, and add them to the mix.

Step 6: Chop Carrots

Now for the finishing touch! Take your Tamari Sauce, or whatever sauce is best to your taste, and spread it on your mix!
